About

I work at the intersection of atmospheric electricity, data science, and instrumentation. My research combines multi-instrument field observations — field mills, particle detectors, and weather radar — with long-term time-series analysis to understand how local and global processes shape the near-surface electric field. I also teach Python and machine learning for environmental applications.
